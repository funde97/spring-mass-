2. Solve the differential equation for different damping conditions.

Theory

Spring Mass System using Differential Equations (Constant Damping Coefficient)

The spring mass system is an important application of second-order differential equations. It is widely used to study vibrations, oscillations, and damping in mechanical systems. In this topic, the motion of the system is represented mathematically using differential equations and solved to determine displacement, velocity, and behavior of vibration with time.

1. Components of Spring Mass System

A damped spring mass system consists of three key components:

Mass (m)

Object attached to the spring; provides inertia to the system

Spring (k)

Provides restoring force proportional to displacement

Damper (c)

Opposes motion and reduces vibration over time

2. Forces Acting on the System

According to Newton's Second Law, the net force equals mass times acceleration: \( \text{Net Force} = m\dfrac{d^2x}{dt^2} \)

Three forces act on the mass:

(a) Spring Force

Using Hooke's Law, the spring force is:

\[ F_s = -kx \]

where \(k\) = spring constant and \(x\) = displacement. This force acts opposite to displacement.

(b) Damping Force

The damping force is proportional to velocity:

\[ F_d = -c\frac{dx}{dt} \]

where \(c\) = damping coefficient and \(\dfrac{dx}{dt}\) = velocity. This force opposes motion.

(c) Inertia Force

Due to acceleration of mass:

\[ F_i = m\frac{d^2x}{dt^2} \]
3. Formation of Differential Equation

Applying Newton's second law and summing all forces:

\[ m\frac{d^2x}{dt^2} = -c\frac{dx}{dt} - kx \]

Rearranging gives the general differential equation of damped free vibration:

\[ m\frac{d^2x}{dt^2} + c\frac{dx}{dt} + kx = 0 \]

This equation is:

Second-order Linear Homogeneous Constant coefficient
4. Standard Form

Dividing throughout by \(m\):

\[ \frac{d^2x}{dt^2} + \frac{c}{m}\frac{dx}{dt} + \frac{k}{m}x = 0 \]

Let \(2\alpha = \dfrac{c}{m}\) and \(\omega_n^2 = \dfrac{k}{m}\), then:

\[ \frac{d^2x}{dt^2} + 2\alpha\frac{dx}{dt} + \omega_n^2 x = 0 \]

where \(\alpha\) = damping factor and \(\omega_n\) = natural angular frequency.

5. Solution of Differential Equation

Assume solution \(x = e^{rt}\). Then \(\dfrac{dx}{dt} = re^{rt}\) and \(\dfrac{d^2x}{dt^2} = r^2e^{rt}\).

Substituting into the equation and dividing by \(e^{rt} \neq 0\) gives the auxiliary (characteristic) equation:

\[ r^2 + 2\alpha r + \omega_n^2 = 0 \]
6. Roots of Auxiliary Equation

Using the quadratic formula:

\[ r = -\alpha \pm \sqrt{\alpha^2 - \omega_n^2} \]

The nature of the roots determines the system's behavior.

7. Cases of Damping

Case 1: Overdamped System

Condition: \(\alpha^2 > \omega_n^2\)

Roots are real and distinct (\(r_1, r_2 \in \mathbb{R}\)).

Solution:

\[ x = C_1 e^{r_1 t} + C_2 e^{r_2 t} \]

Characteristics: No oscillation. Motion slowly returns to equilibrium. Heavy damping.

Case 2: Critically Damped System

Condition: \(\alpha^2 = \omega_n^2\)

Roots are equal: \(r = -\alpha\).

Solution:

\[ x = (C_1 + C_2 t)\, e^{-\alpha t} \]

Characteristics: Fastest return to equilibrium. No oscillation.

Applications: Door closers, measuring instruments.

Case 3: Underdamped System

Condition: \(\alpha^2 < \omega_n^2\)

Roots are complex: \(r = -\alpha \pm i\beta\), where \(\beta = \sqrt{\omega_n^2 - \alpha^2}\).

Solution:

\[ x = e^{-\alpha t}(C_1 \cos\beta t + C_2 \sin\beta t) \]

Characteristics: Oscillatory motion. Amplitude gradually decreases. Most practical systems are underdamped.

8. Undamped System

If damping coefficient \(c = 0\):

\[ \frac{d^2x}{dt^2} + \omega_n^2 x = 0 \]

Auxiliary equation: \(r^2 + \omega_n^2 = 0\), giving roots \(r = \pm\, i\omega_n\).

General Solution:

\[ x = C_1\cos\omega_n t + C_2\sin\omega_n t \]

This represents continuous simple harmonic motion.

9. Physical Interpretation

• The spring stores energy.

• The damper dissipates energy.

• The mass provides inertia.

• Differential equations describe how displacement changes with time.

10. Importance in Mathematics

This topic helps in understanding:

11. Applications
Vehicle suspension systems Earthquake vibration analysis Robotics Mechanical structures Control systems Aerospace engineering Electrical analog circuits
12. Conclusion

The spring mass system is one of the most important applications of differential equations in engineering and mathematics. Using second-order linear differential equations with constant coefficients, the motion of vibrating systems can be modeled and analyzed accurately.

The damping coefficient determines whether the motion is oscillatory or non-oscillatory, while the mathematical solution depends on the roots of the auxiliary equation.
